Debriefing

Thank you for your participation in our study!

During this study, we are looking to understand how people think about and talk about scientific findings. We are interested to see how people understand science (and especially the science of human behavior) and then share their understanding with others. In other words, we want to see how people who read a news article about science will summarize what they read to another person, and how the person who reads the summary will then explain it to another person, and so on.

The summary that you wrote may be shown to other participants, but they will not be shown your answers to the questionnaire that you took after writing the summary.

As we explained in the consent form, as part of increasing efforts towards transparency and open science in psychology, de-identified data from participants in this study will be shared on a data-sharing service after the study is completed. The sharing of de-identified and non-sensitive data from experiments is becoming a standard in this research area as a way of increasing confidence in scientific findings. Again, this means that any data shared on this service will not be connected in any way to your name or MTurk worker ID.

If you have any questions about this research or would like to have your data withheld from the study, you may contact lead researcher Rachel Jansen at racheljansen@berkeley.edu.
